Step by Step

Processing Explained

Dive into each stage of our workflow — from grey fabric intake to finished, dispatched goods.

Bleaching Process

A well-bleached fabric ensures uniform dye uptake and superior print definition. We use hydrogen peroxide-based bleaching with careful alkali management and full environmental compliance.

1

Grey Fabric Preparation

Fabric desized and scoured to remove warp sizing, oils, and natural impurities before bleaching.

2

Bleach Bath Application

H₂O₂ applied at controlled concentrations (3–5 g/L) with alkali stabilisers at 95–100°C for 45–60 minutes.

3

Neutralisation & Hot Wash

Thoroughly washed and neutralised to remove all bleach chemical traces before further processing.

4

Whiteness Assessment

Whiteness index measured via spectrophotometer. Batches below specification are re-bleached.

Dyeing Process

Colour accuracy is our most critical deliverable. Our computerised colour-matching laboratory guarantees shade consistency across every batch, from small run to bulk order.

1

Lab Dip Development

Small sample dips developed and approved by the client before bulk production begins.

2

Recipe Calculation

Computerised software generates the precise dye recipe — percentages, auxiliaries, salt and alkali ratios.

3

Bulk Dyeing

Fabric loaded into jet, winch, jigger, or HTHP machine. Temperature and pH follow a programmed ramp.

4

After-treatment & Washing

Soaping, washing, and softening applied. Final shade checked against approved standard on spectrophotometer.

Printing Process

From single-colour block prints to 12-colour photographic repeats, our printing facility handles the full range of pattern complexities with speed and precision.

1

Screen / Film Preparation

Artwork separated into colour films. Screens exposed, developed, and hardened for production.

2

Paste Mixing

Print pastes prepared to exact colour formulations. Viscosity adjusted for fabric type and print method.

3

Printing & Drying

Fabric printed on flat-bed or rotary table, then passed through thermofix or steam fixation.

4

Washing & Finishing

Fabric washed to remove unfixed dye and thickener. Softener applied. Final print inspection carried out.

Finishing Process

Finishing defines how a fabric looks, feels, and performs. Our finishing range handles everything from simple softening to complex functional coatings for performance textiles.

1

Chemical Finishing

Softeners or functional chemicals applied via padding mangle at defined pick-up percentages.

2

Curing / Drying

Fabric passed through stenter at precise temperatures to cure chemicals and set fabric dimensions.

3

Mechanical Finishing

Calendering, sanforizing, or emerising applied as required for the target handle and appearance.

4

Width & GSM Check

Final width, GSM, and shrinkage verified against specifications before batching and dispatch.

Quality Assurance

Our in-house quality lab is equipped with industry-standard instruments. Every processed lot is tested before it leaves our facility — no exceptions, no shortcuts.

1

Shade Measurement

Spectrophotometer readings taken. ΔE calculated against approved standard — pass criteria ΔE ≤ 0.8.

2

Fastness Testing

Wash (ISO 105-C06), rub (ISO 105-X12), and light fastness (ISO 105-B02) tested per lot.

3

Physical Testing

GSM, width, tensile strength, tear strength, and dimensional stability checked on every lot.

4

Certification & Dispatch

Passing lots receive a quality certificate with test results. Non-conforming lots are reprocessed.
